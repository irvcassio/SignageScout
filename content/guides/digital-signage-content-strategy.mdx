The most expensive mistake in digital signage is investing in hardware and software, then showing bad content. A $3,000 commercial display showing a stretched PowerPoint slide delivers zero ROI. Content strategy determines whether your signage investment succeeds or fails.

## Content Types That Work

### Information Displays

Show useful, time-sensitive information that changes frequently:
- **Wayfinding** — Building directories, floor maps, room schedules
- **Queue status** — Wait times, now serving numbers
- **Schedules** — Event calendars, meeting room availability, transit departures
- **Metrics** — KPI dashboards, sales leaderboards, production statistics

Information content works because it provides immediate utility. Viewers seek it out rather than ignore it.

### Promotional Content

Drive action with targeted messaging:
- **Product promotions** — Time-limited offers, new arrivals, seasonal campaigns
- **Menu boards** — Food items with pricing, dayparted for meal times
- **Upsell prompts** — Complementary products, add-ons, upgrades
- **Social proof** — Customer reviews, ratings, user-generated content

Promotional content must be visually compelling and change frequently. Stale promotions train viewers to ignore your screens.

### Brand and Culture

Reinforce organizational identity:
- **Employee recognition** — Work anniversaries, achievements, new hires
- **Company news** — Product launches, milestones, press coverage
- **Values and mission** — Rotating brand messaging
- **Social feeds** — Curated Instagram, X, or LinkedIn posts

### Emergency and Safety

Override all other content when needed:
- **Emergency alerts** — Fire, weather, security incidents
- **Safety reminders** — PPE requirements, evacuation routes, COVID protocols

## Design Best Practices

### The 3-Second Rule

Viewers see your screen for 3-5 seconds. Every piece of content must communicate its message in that time.

- **One message per screen** — Do not combine a promotion with an event listing
- **Large text** — Minimum 24pt font, ideally 36pt+ for headlines
- **High contrast** — Dark text on light backgrounds, or light text on dark backgrounds
- **Minimal text** — 5-7 words per headline, 15-20 words maximum per screen

### Layout Zones

Divide your screen into purpose-built zones:

- **Primary zone (60-70% of screen)** — Main content: promotion, menu, or information
- **Ticker zone (10-15%)** — Scrolling text for news, weather, or social feeds
- **Brand zone (10-15%)** — Logo, tagline, or time/date
- **Accent zone (5-10%)** — Decorative elements, seasonal themes

Most CMS platforms ([ScreenCloud](/vendors/screencloud), [Mvix](/vendors/mvix), [NoviSign](/vendors/novisign)) include multi-zone layout editors.

### Color and Typography

- Use your brand colors consistently across all content
- Sans-serif fonts read better on screens at distance (Inter, Helvetica, Open Sans)
- Never use more than 2 fonts per screen
- White space is not wasted space — it improves readability

### Motion and Animation

- Subtle animations attract attention without being distracting
- Avoid rapid transitions, flashing, or aggressive motion
- Slide transitions should be 0.5-1 second maximum
- Auto-play video should be short (15-30 seconds) and silent unless audio is appropriate

## Scheduling Strategy

### Dayparting

Show different content based on time of day:
- **Morning** — Welcome messages, daily schedule, breakfast menu
- **Midday** — Lunch specials, afternoon events, meeting reminders
- **Evening** — End-of-day announcements, after-hours information

### Content Rotation

- **Dwell time** — Each content item should display for 8-15 seconds
- **Playlist length** — 6-10 items per rotation (60-150 second full loop)
- **Refresh frequency** — Update at least weekly to prevent "content blindness"

### Event-Triggered Content

Advanced CMS platforms can trigger content based on external data:
- Weather changes — promote hot drinks when temperature drops
- Inventory levels — switch promotions when stock is low
- Time-based — happy hour pricing, lunch rush content
- Audience — demographic-triggered content with camera analytics

Every major CMS platform includes template libraries:
- [Rise Vision](/vendors/rise-vision) — 500+ free templates with Google Workspace integration
- [Mvix](/vendors/mvix) — 500+ templates with data binding
- [NoviSign](/vendors/novisign) — Drag-and-drop template editor
- [Yodeck](/vendors/yodeck) — Template library with Unsplash integration

Templates are the fastest path to decent-looking content. Customize colors and fonts to match your brand, replace placeholder text, and publish.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too much text** — If it takes more than 5 seconds to read, it is too long
2. **Low resolution images** — Stretched, pixelated images destroy credibility
3. **Static content** — Content that never changes becomes invisible within days
4. **No content owner** — Without a designated person responsible, content stagnates
5. **Ignoring the environment** — A brightly lit retail window needs 700+ nit brightness; a dark lobby needs lower brightness to avoid glare
6. **No call to action** — Promotional content without a clear "what to do next" wastes the impression

## Measuring Content Effectiveness

Track these metrics to improve your content over time:

- **Dwell time** — How long do viewers look at each content item? (requires camera analytics)
- **Engagement** — For interactive displays, track touch interactions per content item
- **Conversion** — For promotions, track uplift in sales during display periods
- **Content freshness** — Track how often each screen's content is updated
