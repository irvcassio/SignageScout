Schools and universities are one of the fastest-growing markets for digital signage. From campus directories to emergency notifications, digital displays improve communication across sprawling campuses with diverse audiences — students, faculty, staff, and visitors.

## Common Use Cases

### Campus Directories and Wayfinding

University campuses with dozens of buildings need wayfinding at key intersections, building entrances, and parking structures. Interactive kiosks help visitors find offices, classrooms, and event venues.

### Event and Schedule Boards

Digital displays in student centers, libraries, and dining halls show daily events, club meetings, athletic schedules, and academic deadlines. Content updates instantly across all locations.

### Emergency Notifications

Digital signage integrates with campus emergency alert systems (like Rave Mobile Safety or Everbridge) to display lockdown instructions, severe weather warnings, and evacuation routes. Screens override normal content immediately when an alert triggers.

### Classroom and Meeting Room Signs

Small displays outside classrooms and conference rooms show current and upcoming reservations, room availability, and wayfinder information. [Visix](/vendors/visix) specializes in room sign solutions for education.

### Cafeteria Menu Boards

Digital menu boards in dining halls show daily menus, allergen information, nutritional data, and specials. Dayparting automatically switches between breakfast, lunch, and dinner menus.

### Athletics and Donor Recognition

Lobbies of athletic facilities use digital displays for donor walls, team schedules, live scores, and recruitment content.

## Why Education Loves Digital Signage

1. **Reach a mobile audience** — Students do not read email. Screens in high-traffic areas reach them where they already are.
2. **Instant updates** — Snow days, room changes, and event cancellations push to every screen in seconds.
3. **Reduce print costs** — Universities spend thousands annually on printed flyers, posters, and banners.
4. **Emergency readiness** — Compliance with Clery Act and campus safety requirements.

## Content Strategy for Schools

1. **Centralized management** — One person (or small team) manages all campus screens from a single dashboard
2. **Departmental access** — Give departments the ability to submit content for their building's screens, with approval workflow
3. **Template library** — Use CMS templates for events, announcements, and alerts so non-designers can create content
4. **Automated feeds** — Pull from Google Calendar, social media, RSS, and campus event systems
5. **Emergency override** — Configure emergency content to instantly replace all normal content
