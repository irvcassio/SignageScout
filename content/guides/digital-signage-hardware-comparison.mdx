Digital signage hardware falls into three categories: displays (what viewers see), media players (what drives the content), and infrastructure (mounts, enclosures, networking). Getting the hardware right is critical — it determines reliability, image quality, and total cost of ownership.

## Commercial Displays vs Consumer TVs

The most common mistake in digital signage is using consumer televisions. Here is why commercial displays are worth the premium:

| Feature | Consumer TV | Commercial Display |
|---------|------------|-------------------|
| Operating Hours | 4-8 hours/day | 16-24 hours/day |
| Brightness | 250-400 nits | 500-2,500 nits |
| Lifespan | 2-3 years | 5-7 years |
| Warranty | 1 year | 3-5 years |
| Orientation | Landscape only | Landscape and portrait |
| Remote Management | None | Built-in (RS232, LAN, IP) |
| Built-in Player | Smart TV (limited) | SoC with CMS support |

A $400 consumer TV that fails after 18 months costs more than a $1,200 commercial display that runs for 5 years.

## Top Display Manufacturers

### Samsung Business Displays

[Samsung](/vendors/samsung-business) offers the broadest product range in commercial displays. Their lineup includes:

- **QBR/QMR Series** — Standard commercial displays (500 nits, 16/7 operation)
- **OMN/OHN Series** — Outdoor displays (3,000-4,000 nits, IP56 rated)
- **The Wall** — MicroLED video walls for premium installations
- **Transparent OLED** — Retail storefront displays

Samsung's built-in Tizen SoC (System-on-Chip) means you can run CMS software directly on the display without an external media player, reducing hardware costs and points of failure.

### LG Business Solutions

[LG](/vendors/lg-business) competes head-to-head with Samsung and leads in OLED technology:

- **UH5N/UH7J Series** — Standard commercial displays
- **OLED Signage** — Thin, vibrant panels for premium retail and hospitality
- **Transparent OLED** — Industry-leading transparent display technology
- **Ultra Stretch** — Ultra-wide displays for shelving and architectural applications

LG's webOS platform is built into their commercial displays. SuperSign CMS supports up to 1,000 displays from a single management console.

### NEC/Sharp NEC Display Solutions

[Sharp NEC](/vendors/nec-display) is known for enterprise reliability:

- **M/MA/ME Series** — Commercial displays with OPS (Open Pluggable Specification) expansion slot
- **Direct View LED** — Large-format LED walls
- **Projectors** — Laser projectors for large venue signage

The OPS slot lets you upgrade the built-in computer module without replacing the entire display — a unique advantage for long-term deployments.

### Planar (Leyard)

[Planar](/vendors/planar) focuses on premium, high-performance installations:

- **DirectLight LED** — Fine-pitch LED video walls (down to 0.7mm pixel pitch)
- **Clarity Matrix** — LCD video walls with ultra-narrow bezels
- **Touch Screens** — Interactive displays for kiosks and wayfinding

Planar is the go-to choice for mission-critical displays in control rooms, corporate lobbies, and broadcast environments.

## Media Players

For displays without a built-in SoC (or when you need more processing power), a standalone media player drives the content.

### BrightSign

[BrightSign](/vendors/brightsign) dominates the dedicated media player market:

- **HD5 Series** — Entry-level, 4K playback
- **XD5 Series** — Mid-range, HTML5 + live TV input
- **XT5 Series** — High-performance, 8K capable, dual HDMI output
- **LS5 Series** — Ultra-compact, affordable for simple deployments

BrightSign players are solid-state (no moving parts, no fans), boot in seconds, and are purpose-built for 24/7 signage operation.

### SpinetiX

[SpinetiX](/vendors/spinetix) offers Swiss-engineered media players (HMP series) with an embedded OS optimized for signage. Known for extreme reliability and security. Their ARYA cloud CMS provides a complete end-to-end solution.

### System-on-Chip (SoC) Built-in Players

Samsung (Tizen), LG (webOS), and Philips (Android SoC) build media players directly into their commercial displays. This eliminates a separate hardware device and reduces points of failure. Most leading CMS platforms (ScreenCloud, Signagelive, NoviSign) support these built-in players.

**When to use SoC vs external player:**
- **SoC:** Simple content (images, video, basic HTML5), cost-sensitive, fewer devices to manage
- **External player:** Complex HTML5, live data feeds, multi-zone layouts, 8K content, interactive touch

## Mounting and Infrastructure

[Legrand AV](/vendors/legrand-av) (Chief, Da-Lite, Vaddio) is the industry standard for signage mounting hardware:

- **Wall mounts** — Fixed, tilt, and articulating options for every display size
- **Ceiling mounts** — Suspended displays for retail, airports, and open environments
- **Kiosk enclosures** — Freestanding enclosures for interactive displays
- **Video wall mounts** — Precision mounting systems for seamless video walls
- **Outdoor enclosures** — Weather-sealed cases for using indoor displays outdoors

## Large-Format LED

For very large displays (over 100 inches), direct-view LED is increasingly preferred over LCD video walls:

[Daktronics](/vendors/daktronics) is the market leader for large-format outdoor LED — think billboards, sports stadiums, and transit systems. For indoor fine-pitch LED, Planar and Samsung lead the market.
