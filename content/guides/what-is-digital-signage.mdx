Digital signage is any electronically controlled display that shows information, advertising, or other content. If you have seen a screen showing a menu at a fast-food restaurant, a directory in an office lobby, or a promotional display in a retail store, you have seen digital signage.

## How Digital Signage Works

A digital signage system has three components:

1. **Display** — A commercial-grade screen (LCD, LED, or OLED) mounted in a visible location
2. **Media Player** — A small computer that sends content to the display. This can be a dedicated device (like [BrightSign](/vendors/brightsign)) or a chip built into the display itself (like Samsung's Tizen SoC)
3. **Content Management System (CMS)** — Software that lets you create, schedule, and manage what appears on screen. Platforms like [ScreenCloud](/vendors/screencloud) and [Rise Vision](/vendors/rise-vision) provide this

You create content in the CMS, schedule when it should appear, and the CMS pushes it to the media player, which displays it on screen. Most modern CMS platforms are cloud-based, so you can manage screens anywhere with an internet connection.

## Where Digital Signage Is Used

### Retail

In-store promotions, window displays, digital price tags, and product information screens. Digital signage in retail increases impulse purchases and allows instant promotion updates across all locations.

### Corporate

Lobby directories, meeting room signs, employee communications, KPI dashboards, and visitor welcome screens. Companies use platforms like [Four Winds Interactive](/vendors/four-winds-interactive) and [Poppulo](/vendors/poppulo) for workplace communications.

### Healthcare

Patient wayfinding, waiting room information, emergency alerts, and staff communications. Hospitals require HIPAA-aware solutions and integration with electronic health record systems.

### Education

Campus directories, event boards, cafeteria menus, emergency notifications, and classroom schedules. [Rise Vision](/vendors/rise-vision) and [Visix](/vendors/visix) are popular in educational settings due to free or education-priced tiers.

### Quick-Service Restaurants

Digital menu boards are the most common QSR application. They enable dayparting (different menus for breakfast, lunch, dinner), dynamic pricing, and upsell prompts. [CrownTV](/vendors/crowntv) offers turnkey QSR solutions.

### Transportation

Flight information displays (FIDS), departure boards, wayfinding in airports and train stations, and real-time transit information. [Daktronics](/vendors/daktronics) and [Broadsign](/vendors/broadsign) serve this market.

### Hospitality

Hotel lobby directories, event schedules, restaurant menus, and guest-facing information displays.

## Digital Signage vs Static Signs

| | Static Signs | Digital Signage |
|-|-------------|----------------|
| Update speed | Days to weeks (print, ship, install) | Minutes (push from CMS) |
| Content variety | One message per sign | Unlimited rotating content |
| Time-based content | Not possible | Automatic dayparting |
| Multi-location updates | Ship to each location | One click, all locations |
| Ongoing cost | Recurring print costs | One-time hardware + monthly software |
| Engagement | Low | 400% more views than static displays |
| Data and analytics | None | Views, dwell time, interaction tracking |

## How Much Does Digital Signage Cost?

**Entry level (1 screen):**
- Display: $300-800 (consumer TV for non-critical use)
- Media Player: $50-100 (Raspberry Pi + [Yodeck](/vendors/yodeck) free plan)
- Software: $0-15/month
- **Total: Under $500 to start**

**Professional (5-20 screens):**
- Displays: $800-1,500 each (commercial grade)
- Media Players: $200-400 each ([BrightSign](/vendors/brightsign) HD series)
- Software: $15-30/screen/month
- Installation: $200-500/screen
- **Total: $1,500-2,500 per screen all-in**

**Enterprise (50+ screens):**
- Custom pricing from vendors
- Volume discounts on hardware and software
- Managed services available from vendors like [Spectrio](/vendors/spectrio)
- **Typical: $10-25/screen/month for software, plus hardware**

## Getting Started

1. **Define your goal** — What problem are you solving? Wayfinding? Promotions? Employee communications?
2. **Count your screens** — How many locations, how many screens per location?
3. **Set a budget** — Include hardware, software, installation, and content creation
4. **Evaluate vendors** — [Browse our directory](/vendors) to find vendors that match your industry and requirements
5. **Start small** — Pilot with 1-3 screens before committing to a full rollout
